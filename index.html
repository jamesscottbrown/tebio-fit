Posterior Distribution

Epsilon values:

This is a matrix of scatterplots of samples from the posterior distribution.

Each point corresponds to a choice of parameters that, when simulated, gave a time-series that was "close" to the provided experimental data. In this context, two time-series are considered "close" if the Euclidian distance between them is less than the user-specified parameter epsilon.

If uniform priors are applied, the axes are scaled to the range of the prior. The proportion of the area covered by the samples thus gives an indication of the factor by which uncertainty in the parameters has been reduced by the experimental observations.
